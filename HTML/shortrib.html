Beef Short Ribs & Creamy Mash

Ingredients:

Short Ribs

  • 4 beef short ribs
  • 1 1/2 tsp each salt and pepper
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 3 garlic cloves, crushed
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 2 celery ribs, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 cups dry red wine (cabaret sauvignon or merlot)
  • 2 cups beef stock/broth, low sodium
  • 2 sprigs thyme (optional)
  • 2 bay leaves

Creamy Mash

  • 4 lbs russet potatoes, peeled
  • 1 1/4 cup hot milk
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp chives, chopped

Instructions:

Short Ribs

  1. Preheat oven to 325Â°F.
  2. Sprinkle beef all over with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat oil in a large ovenproof pot over high heat. Add half the ribs and brown aggressively all over (5 to 7 min in total). Remove and repeat with remaining ribs, then remove.
  4. Turn heat down to medium. Add onion and garlic into the same pot and cook for 2 minutes.
  5. Add carrot and celery, cook for 5 minutes until carrot is softened and sweet.
  6. Add tomato paste and cook for 1 minute.
  7. Add wine, broth, thyme and bay leaves. Stir until tomato paste is dissolved.
  8. Return beef into liquid, arranging them so they are submerged.
  9. Cover with lid and transfer to oven for 3 hours, or until the meat can easily be pried apart with forks.
  10. Remove beef carefully, keeping the meat on the bone. Cover to keep warm.
  11. Strain all liquid in the pot, pressing juices out of the onion, carrot etc. (Optional - can skip if you don't mind chunky sauce) Return sauce into pot, bring to simmer and stir. Adjust as necessary - simmer to reduce/thicken, add water to thin, season with salt and pepper if needed.
  12. Place beef on serving plate, spoon over sauce. Serve!

Creamy Mash

  1. Peel potatoes. Submerge in cold water (enough to cover poatoes). Bring to a boil and cook until easily peirced with a fork (20 to 25 mins, depending on size).
  2. Drain well and tranfer to the bowl of stand mixer. Use whisk attachment and lightly mash potatoes by hand to break them up. Attach whisk and start mixer on low speed for 30 seconds. Increase speed to medium and slowly dirzzle in hot milk.
  3. While mixer is running add butter 1 tbsp at a time, waiting a few seconds between each addition. Poatoes should be whipped and fluffy. Add salt to taste.
  4. Add creamy mash to plate and top with finely chopped chives for garnish.
